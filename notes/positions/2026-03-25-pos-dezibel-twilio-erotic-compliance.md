## Context

Twilio's Acceptable Use Policy prohibits SHAFT content (Sex, Hate, Alcohol, Firearms, Tobacco) on standard messaging. Dezibel's P/F novella contains explicit sexual content. If Twilio classifies this as SHAFT-Sex, standard long codes and toll-free numbers won't work. Options: dedicated short code ($500-1000/month, 8-12 week carrier approval), alternative SMS provider, or iMessage-first delivery (bypasses carrier entirely for Apple users).

This is a potential launch blocker. The iMessage-first strategy mitigates it (iMessage doesn't route through carriers), but Android users need SMS fallback.

## Evidence So Far

- Legal research confirms SHAFT policy applies to standard Twilio messaging
- Dedicated short code is the compliant path for regulated content ($500-1000/month)
- iMessage delivery is free and unregulated by carriers (Apple-to-Apple)
- No confirmation from Twilio on whether literary erotic fiction triggers SHAFT enforcement
