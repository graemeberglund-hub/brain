## Thesis

Before spending any marketing budget, send the 3-day SMS demo to 100 people and measure how many want the remaining 39 days. This is the single cheapest validation of product-market fit. If conversion is high (50%+), the product sells itself and marketing spend can be lean. If conversion is low, no amount of marketing budget fixes a product people don't want to continue.

Every successful category creator in the research (Serial, Wordle, Calm) validated demand with the cheapest possible test before spending. Calm built "Do Nothing for Two Minutes" before building the app. Wordle had zero marketing budget — the product was the marketing.

## Evidence For

- Category creation research: every precedent validated cheaply first
- Cohort completion rates (70-96%) suggest strong retention IF people start
- Day 1 demo already functional — cost of running 3-day test is near zero
- 3-day demo gives real data for investor conversations (conversion rate, engagement metrics, qualitative feedback)
- Budget audit: this test de-risks the entire $511k Stage 1 spend
- [[kill-test-full-project]] — A3 finds retention UNVERIFIED, reinforcing urgency of running the demo test this position advocates (2026-03-26)
- [[kill-test-full-project]] — A7 downgrades Guardian from 390 to 200-300 subscribers, making demo validation even more critical (2026-03-26)

## Evidence Against

- 100 people may not be statistically significant
- Selection bias: first 100 testers are likely friends/network (warmer audience than market)
- 3 days may not represent the 42-day commitment (people may convert at 3 days but churn at day 14)
