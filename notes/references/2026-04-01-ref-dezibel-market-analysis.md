## 1. Market Sizing

- **TAM — $12.2B.** Global web fiction platforms ($10.8B, 2024) + audio erotica/wellness (~$1.4B slice of romance ebook market). Dezibel sits at the intersection of serialized fiction delivery and premium erotic literary experience.
- **SAM — $1.6B.** English-language romance/erotica ebooks ($1.44B) + audio erotica subscriptions (~$150M, derived from Quinn's $11M ARR at <1% penetration of the romance reader base of ~30M US adults). This is the audience that already pays for intimate literary content digitally.
- **SOM — $15-45M (Year 1-2).** At $49-159 ASP across 3 tiers, 100K-300K subscribers over 2 years yields $5-48M. Conservative SOM assumes 100K subs at $49 blended = $4.9M; optimistic assumes 150K at $100 blended = $15M. For reference, Quinn hit $11M ARR with $3.2M raised; Dipsea plateaued at $6M ARR with $15M raised.

*Assumptions: English-language markets only. No format licensing or film IP revenue included. Subscriber projections benchmarked against Quinn's growth curve (440% YoY) applied to Dezibel's higher price point but narrower initial catalog (1 title vs. library).*

## 2. Top 5 Demand Trends

- **Romance is eating publishing.** Romance doubled in volume 2020-2024; romantasy alone grew 34% YoY to $610M (2024, Bloomberg). Romance = 58% of all Amazon ebook sales.
- **Audio-first erotica is the breakout format.** Quinn hit $11M+ ARR with 440% YoY growth on just $3.2M raised — the only profitable growth story in the space while Radish ($440M acquisition) shut down and Kindle Vella folded.
- **Chat fiction dramatically outperforms traditional text.** Hooked's A/B tests: teenagers who couldn't finish 1,000-word excerpts of bestsellers read equivalent-length stories formatted as text conversations to completion.
- **SMS engagement dwarfs every other channel.** 98% open rate (vs. email 15-25%), 21-35% CTR (vs. email 2.5%). No published data on SMS-delivered fiction specifically — Dezibel would be first-mover with measurable engagement data.
- **Connected device integration is mainstream.** Bluetooth-enabled toys = 38.5% of sextech revenue ($16.4B of $42.6B market). The hardware attach rate is proven; the content-to-device pipeline is not.

## 3. Top 5 Underserved Opportunities

- **Premium single-title experience vs. content library.** Every competitor (Quinn, Dipsea, Wattpad, Episode) runs a library subscription model. No one sells a single authored literary work as a 42-day immersive experience at experience pricing ($49-199). The concert model, not the Spotify model.
- **SMS-as-narrative-medium, not marketing channel.** Attentive ($7B valuation), Postscript ($65M Series C), Community.com ($500M+) all proved SMS economics — but solely for marketing. Zero companies deliver fiction through SMS triggers. The engagement data would be proprietary and defensible.
- **Content-to-haptic synchronization.** Lovense dominates teledildonics hardware (~$100M+ revenue) but has no premium literary content partner. Dipsea explored audio-haptic but was acquired before shipping it. The BLE-to-narrative-beat integration is an open lane.
- **Female-gaze erotic literary for 25-45 demo.** Quinn skews 56% aged 18-24. The 25-45 cohort — higher disposable income, BookTok-adjacent, established romance readers — has no premium product designed for them. Dezibel's price point and literary positioning targets exactly this gap.
- **Post-library market for serialized fiction.** Radish (dead), Kindle Vella (dead), Wattpad (MAU declining 10.5% YoY). The micropayment/ad-supported model failed. The market is open for premium-priced, author-driven serialized work that treats scarcity as a feature.

## 4. Follow the Money

- **AI-powered interactive narrative — $200M+ deployed (2023-2024).** Character.AI ($150M Series A, then $2.5B Google deal), Inworld AI ($50M), Hidden Door ($4.6M). Investors are betting on conversational/character-driven experiences. Dezibel's real-time SMS delivery creates perceived AI-like intimacy without the AI — a positioning advantage as AI backlash grows.
- **Sextech hardware + software convergence — $42.6B market, 16.7% CAGR.** Lovehoney Group valued at $1.2B (2021, Telemos Capital). Capital is flowing into hardware but the content layer remains unbundled. First company to own content-to-device pipeline captures integration premium.
- **SMS infrastructure at scale — $7B+ in cumulative funding.** Attentive ($7B), Postscript ($65M), Community.com ($500M+). The pipes are funded and proven. What's missing is a consumer product that uses SMS for content delivery rather than conversion marketing.
- **Romance/BookTok-driven publishing — fastest-growing book category.** Romance volume doubled in 4 years. Romantasy $610M and growing 34% YoY. No VC-backed pure-play has captured this audience in a format native to how they already consume (phone-first, chat-native). Traditional publishers are watching but not building.

## Key Signal

The 2021 acquisition spree ($1.55B across Wattpad, Radish, Tapas) has largely failed. Radish dead, Vella dead, Wattpad declining. The micropayment/ad-supported library model is the failure mode. Quinn ($11M ARR on $3.2M raised) is the outlier — premium subscription, female-gaze, audio-first. Dezibel's model (premium pricing, single-title experience, multi-sensory) aligns with what's working, not what's dying.
