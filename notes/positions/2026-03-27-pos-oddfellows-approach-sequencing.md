## Context

Oddfellows Pictures (best friends, $200M+ global ticket sales) is the natural producer for the dezibel sequel "That Night" as a film. The question was when and how to approach them.

## Options

1. Pitch now with projections and the concept
2. Wait until early sales prove the format, then approach with data + developed treatment

## Decision

Option 2. Approach Oddfellows post-launch (estimated Month 2-3) when subscriber numbers and retention data exist. The treatment for "That Night" exists in pieces but needs assembly into a filmable package before the conversation. Proof beats pitch — impress with results, not projections.

## Consequences

- The film deal ($10-50M revenue lever) is sequenced to Phase 2, not Phase 1
- Phase 1 revenue must come from direct consumer sales + ecosystem products
- Treatment assembly becomes a pre-launch writing task (parallel to Book One completion)
- The pitch is stronger: filmmaker who wrote the IP + built the audience + has the data
- Risk: if launch underperforms, the conversation is harder
