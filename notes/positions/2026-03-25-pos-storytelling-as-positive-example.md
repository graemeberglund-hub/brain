## Thesis

Fiction helps people not by modeling good behavior but by taking readers confidently through difficult material. The author's job is craft mastery over the journey — darkness, complexity, moral ambiguity — so the reader trusts the hand guiding them. Dezibel's real-time intimacy format (iMessage, 42 days) raises the stakes: readers live alongside the story, which makes authorial confidence in the arc non-negotiable. This is not self-help. The help is the experience itself.

## Evidence For

- Stated belief from onboarding conversation (2026-03-25)
- Dezibel's structure delivers story in the same channel as real relationships — the medium itself demands authorial control over emotional trajectory
- The Cheaper Show's ethos (accessibility, lowering barriers) carries forward: art should reach people and change something
- Author's refocus on ensuring the reader is taken confidently through the journey (2026-03-26)

## Evidence Against

- **2026-03-26** (challenge) — Dezibel's governing phrase "ascent as collapse" and character arcs (addiction, violence, manipulation, death) mean the experience is harsh by design; zero reader testing exists to validate whether confident craft guidance registers as intended or causes harm
- iMessage format creates parasocial attachment before protagonist death — craft confidence doesn't eliminate the emotional risk of the medium
