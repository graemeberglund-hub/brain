## Context

Budget audit identified three scenarios: Lean ($350-400k), Full ($725-850k), or Staged ($511k build + $300k+ post-cohort). Staged recommended because: investors pay more for growth capital than build capital, valuation cap improves with subscriber data, and first cohort answers the $100M question with evidence rather than projections.

Key tension: a single raise is simpler operationally but commits $725k+ before any revenue validation. Staged raise requires two fundraising cycles but de-risks the second with real data.

## Evidence So Far

- Budget audit recommends Scenario C (staged) — see `dezibel-budget/analysis/budget-audit.md`
- SAFE notes support staged raises mechanically (each round is a new SAFE)
- Category creation research shows every successful precedent validated demand cheaply before spending marketing budget
- 15,000 subscribers needed to break even on $725k; staged raise reduces breakeven to ~10,000 on $511k
