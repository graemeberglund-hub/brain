## Thesis

Dezibel the title is the proof of concept. The real business is a label — a platform for messaging-delivered fiction that other authors publish through. Dezibel proves the format, the infrastructure, and the economics. Then the door opens to other titles, other authors, other languages.

The comp is A24, not Penguin. A24 released one film that proved their taste was commercial, then became the brand other filmmakers wanted. Dezibel launches, works, and becomes the label credible authors want to deliver through — because no one else has the infrastructure or the audience.

## What the Label Owns

1. **Multi-channel delivery infrastructure.** WhatsApp, SMS, app, push — built once for Dezibel, reused for every title. Each new story is marginal cost on existing pipes.
2. **Global WhatsApp integration.** 2B+ phones already have the delivery app installed. No downloads, no app store approval, no user acquisition cost for the channel.
3. **Haptic synchronization system.** BLE-to-narrative-beat integration. No other publisher has this.
4. **Cohort delivery engine.** Timed, serialized, scarcity-based release cadence. Everyone starts Day 1 together. The format creates social urgency.
5. **Proprietary engagement data.** Message-level open rates, read timing, completion rates, drop-off points — data no traditional publisher or even digital platform has at this granularity. This compounds with every title and every cohort.
6. **The brand.** Dezibel becomes the mark of quality for this format, the way A24 signals a certain kind of film.

## Revenue Model Shift

| | Single Title | Label |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue per title | $49-199 × subscribers | $49-199 × subscribers × titles/year |
| Author economics | Graeme keeps 100% | Revenue share (70/30 or similar) |
| Marginal cost per title | N/A | One adaptation + QA. Infrastructure exists. |
| Defensibility | One great book | Format + infrastructure + data + brand |
| Investor framing | "A $49 book" | "Distribution layer for a new fiction format" |
| Venture scale | No | Yes |

## What Dezibel Must Prove First

Before the label pitch is credible:

1. **Cohort 1 completion rate.** Do readers finish all 42 days? If drop-off is >50% by Day 21, the format has a retention problem that won't be solved by adding more titles.
2. **Engagement data by channel.** Which delivery channel has highest completion? This determines where the label invests infrastructure.
3. **Unit economics at scale in at least one language.** Prove margins hold at 10K+ subscribers.
4. **Second-author test.** The format must work with someone other than Graeme. If Dezibel only works because of Graeme's specific voice, it's a career, not a platform. This is the hardest test and the most important one.

## What's Already Built When the Label Launches

- Multi-channel delivery infrastructure (WhatsApp, SMS, app, push)
- Global language expansion pipeline (Spanish, Portuguese, French)
- Subscriber base from Dezibel who want the next title
- Proprietary engagement data no publisher has
- Brand association with the format's origin story

## The Investor Conversation

This reframes the raise. The current pitch is a creative project with good economics. The label pitch is a platform play:

> "We're not raising to publish a book. We're raising to prove a format. Dezibel is our launch title. If completion rates hold and unit economics work, we have the distribution layer for a new category of fiction — delivered globally through WhatsApp to 2 billion phones, with engagement data no publisher on earth has. The infrastructure is built once. Every title after that is marginal cost."

This is a venture-scale story. A single title isn't.

## Sequencing

1. Launch Dezibel (English) — prove format, collect data
2. Spanish adaptation — prove global portability
3. Portuguese — prove marginal cost model (same infrastructure, overlapping translator pool)
4. Second author, English — prove format independence from Graeme
5. Announce label — with data from steps 1-4

Step 4 is the gate. Everything before it is building toward the proof. Everything after it is scaling a proven model.
