Cultural products lead with vision → proof of craft → market opportunity. Not problem → solution → metrics. The emotional sequence: make them feel something → show them it's real → show them it's a business.

The deck should feel like the product: beautiful, confident, restrained. Art book layout, not McKinsey. The deck IS a taste test for the product's aesthetic.

The 12 Slides

Slide 1
Title / Hook

Title treatment. One-sentence descriptor. No logo soup, no team photo.

"DEZIBEL — A novel that texts you back."
Job: Intrigue. The investor leans forward. This sentence does the work of a problem slide and a solution slide simultaneously.

Design: should look like a book cover or film poster. Set the tone immediately.

Slide 2
The Experience

What the reader actually experiences. Present tense. Show the phone.

  • You subscribe. The following Sunday at 12:08pm, a text arrives from an unknown number.
  • Over 42 days, a story unfolds — two writers reconnecting after seven years of silence.
  • The messages arrive like real texts. You read them on your phone, in your life, in real time.
  • Video pieces arrive — the actors playing these characters, on film. This is a multimedia literary experience: text, video, audio, haptics.
  • On Day 7, you're offered a wearable device that syncs to the erotic scenes.
  • On Day 36, something happens that changes everything.
Job: Desire. The investor should want to experience this. Not understand it — want it.

Design: show an actual phone screen with a message thread. This slide is the product.

Slide 3
Why Now

Three forces converging:

  1. Attention fatigue is a health crisis. 63% of Gen Z planned a social media detox in 2024. Slow media is a movement: Norwegian Slow TV (3M viewers), Tortoise Media (85K members at £80/year). People are paying for constraint.
  2. Audio erotica proved the market. Quinn: $12M+ ARR, 440% YoY growth, 75%+ women users. Dipsea: acquired at $6M ARR. Women will pay for intimate content framed as wellness, not porn.
  3. No one has combined literary fiction with phone-native delivery. Keitai shosetsu hit 20M readers in Japan's feature phone era. The format works. It just hasn't been done well, in English, with production value.
Job: Inevitability. This isn't a weird idea — it's an obvious idea whose time has come.

Slide 5
Competitive Landscape

2×2 matrix. Axes: Production value (low → high) vs. Delivery intimacy (passive → phone-native).

  • Quinn / Dipsea: Medium production, passive delivery (app, press play)
  • Kindle Vella / Radish: Low production, app-native but not intimate
  • Immersive theatre (Sleep No More): High production, in-person only
  • DEZIBEL: High production, phone-native. The only premium single-title experience delivered through the most intimate screen you own.
"Every library model in this space has died. The market is waiting for a single premium title done right."
Job: Category clarity. Dezibel creates a new quadrant. Show the white space.

Slide 8
Go-to-Market

Specific sequence, not a vague marketing plan:

  3. T-2 weeks: Free Day 1 preview → email capture → nurture (15-25% conversion vs. 2-3% cold).

Job: Sophistication. The launch sequence is designed. The same mind that wrote the novel is running the go-to-market.

Handling Specific Concerns

The erotic content angle

Do not lead with it. Do not hide it.

"The story includes erotic content — it's literary fiction about desire and intimacy, not pornography. The haptic integration is an opt-in enhancement, not the core product. Quinn proved the market: $12M ARR, 75% women, positioned as sexual wellness."

Language to use: "intimate content," "erotic scenes," "sexual wellness." Not: "porn," "adult content," "NSFW."

Solo founder

"This is a writer-director project. I wrote it, I'll direct the trailer, I'm designing the launch. That's not a gap — that's the point. The operations producer is the first hire post-raise, and I have two candidates."

Financial projections

Show unit economics at $49 and $149 (margins), break-even subscriber count (~10,000 on $500K), Quinn as revenue comp ($12M ARR on $3.2M raised — 3.75x return on capital in ARR alone).

One-time purchase, not subscription

"Every library model in serialized fiction has died — Kindle Vella, Radish, Hooked. Dezibel is an experience you buy once, like a concert ticket. $49 for 42 days. The scarcity is real — you were there or you weren't."

"Recurring revenue comes from new titles, new cohorts, the Substack, and ecosystem products."

Pre-Meeting Checklist

  • Demo on your phone — charged, loaded, ready to hand over
  • Deck exported as PDF for forwarding after the meeting
  • Know the SAFE terms cold: $8M cap, rolling, checks from $10K
  • Prepare 2-minute verbal pitch (no slides) for informal settings
  • Quinn numbers memorized: $12M ARR, $3.2M raised, 75% women, 440% YoY
  • Budget breakdown ready if asked (not in the deck, but in your head)
  • Know your "what if this doesn't work" answer: "The novel gets published traditionally. The format research has value. The IP exists regardless."
