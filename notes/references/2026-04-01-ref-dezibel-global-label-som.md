## Bottom-Up SOM Model

| Variable | Year 1 | Year 3 | Year 5 |
|----------|--------|--------|--------|
| Titles | 1 (Dezibel) | 4-6/year | 10-12/year |
| Languages per title | 1 (English) | 3 (EN/ES/PT) | 5 (EN/ES/PT/FR/KO) |
| Subs per title per language | 5-10K | 15-30K | 30-75K |
| Blended ASP | $75 | $65 (lower in LATAM) | $55 (global price tiering) |
| Revenue share to authors | 0% (own title) | 30% on others | 30% on others |

## Revenue Trajectory

| Year | Titles × Languages | Avg Subs | ASP | Gross Revenue | Net (after author share) |
|------|-------------------|----------|-----|---------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | 1 × 1 = 1 | 7,500 | $75 | $562K | $562K |
| 3 | 5 × 3 = 15 | 20,000 | $65 | $19.5M | ~$14M |
| 5 | 12 × 5 = 60 | 50,000 | $55 | $165M | ~$120M |

Note: Year 3 and 5 figures do not include back catalog revenue from previous titles running new cohorts. With back catalog, Year 5 gross could reach $200M+.

## Compounding Effects

1. **Back catalog.** Titles run indefinitely as new cohorts. By Year 5, 30+ titles in 5 languages running rolling cohorts. Revenue = new titles + all previous titles still earning.
2. **Audience carryover.** Readers who finish one title are near-zero CAC for the next. Customer acquisition cost drops with every title while catalog grows.
3. **Data moat.** By Year 3, proprietary data on which story beats, message cadences, and delivery times produce highest completion rates across languages and cultures. No competitor has this.

## Key Gate

Subscriber completion rate from Cohort 1. If 70%+ finish all 42 days, these projections are conservative. If under 40%, the format has a retention problem more titles won't fix.
