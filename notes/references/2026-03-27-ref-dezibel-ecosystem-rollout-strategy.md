### Layer 10: Clandestine Campaign

| Assumption | Kill Test | Cost | Status |
|-----------|----------|------|--------|
| Literary fiction readers decode breadcrumbs and share | Beta reader test — give Day 1 with no context, track sharing | $0 | NOT RUN |
| oBitchuary → Shit Eyes → Hot Ghost → dezibel trail is discoverable | Plant one connection, see if anyone finds it | $0 | NOT RUN |
| The "is it real?" mechanic works for literary fiction | — | — | THEORETICAL |

**Assessment: THIS IS THE HIGHEST-RISK MARKETING BET.**

The comparable data is clear:
- Blair Witch ($248M, 3.2% marketing spend): "is it real?" worked because the medium was new (internet, 1999) and the genre (horror) rewards paranoia
- Longlegs ($128M, 7.8% marketing spend): withholding worked because horror fans are puzzle-solvers who share clues

**The kill question: do literary fiction readers behave this way?**

Horror fans decode. They share clues. They build Reddit threads. Literary fiction readers... recommend books at dinner parties. The sharing mechanic is fundamentally different. Literary fiction spreads through trust networks (friends, bookstores, reviews), not puzzle-solving communities.

**This doesn't kill the clandestine campaign — it reframes it.** The breadcrumbs (oBitchuary, Shit Eyes, Hot Ghost) work as independent cultural objects that create a richer world for readers who ARE already reading dezibel. They're depth, not acquisition. Acquisition comes from: Guardian piece, press, word of mouth, Lovense App Gallery, BookTok.

**Revised marketing strategy:**
1. **Acquisition:** Guardian + press + BookTok + Lovense App Gallery + trailer
2. **Depth:** Clandestine ecosystem (rewards existing readers, creates "cult" layer)
3. **Retention:** 42-day drip structure (built-in)
4. **Viral moments:** Day 7 (vibrator reveal), Day 36 (Emma dies), Day 42 ("Who's this")

**VERDICT: Build the platform modularly from Day 1 so it CAN support multiple stories. Don't commit resources to the label until Book One proves the format. But make the architectural decisions now that don't close this door.**

## VI. WHAT KILLS THIS — THE REAL RISKS

### Risk 1: 42-Day Retention Failure (EXISTENTIAL)
If people don't finish the story, nothing else matters. The product is the experience of receiving texts for 42 days. If Day 7 retention is below 60%, the format doesn't work.
**Mitigation:** Beta test NOW with 5-7 readers. This is the cheapest, most important test.

### Risk 2: Raise Fails or Delays 6+ Months (CRITICAL)
Everything post-raise is sequenced. A 6-month raise delay means a 6-month launch delay, which means ecosystem products go stale (oBitchuary loses momentum, Hot Ghost inventory sits).
**Mitigation:** Run every $0 kill test before raising. Walk into investor meetings with beta data, not projections.

### Risk 3: Twilio/SMS Compliance (CRITICAL)
SHAFT compliance means Twilio may not support erotic content over SMS. Violation fines: up to $1,000/message.
**Mitigation:** Research Voyage SMS (explicitly supports sexual wellness). Resolve BEFORE platform build. The compliance question is a lawyer question — get the lawyer first.

### Risk 4: Jodi Balfour Says No (HIGH)
The Emma voice is central. Jodi + Abbi (married IRL) is a press story. If Jodi passes, the casting search adds 2-3 months.
**Mitigation:** Have 2 backup Emma candidates identified before the Jodi meeting.

### Risk 5: Editor Search Takes 6+ Months (HIGH)
Tier 1 editors (Sarah McGrath, Mitzi Angel) may not respond or may be booked. The editor is on the critical path to quality.
**Mitigation:** Start with Tier 2 (Liz Johnston — BC roots, accessible). Tier 1 is aspirational; Tier 2 is sufficient.

### Risk 6: Android Experience Degrades Below Acceptable (MEDIUM)
~45% of the market gets SMS instead of iMessage. No read receipts, no typing indicators, degraded media. If the Android experience is bad, you lose half your addressable market.
**Mitigation:** Test on Android NOW. If degraded, consider: (a) web app fallback for Android users, (b) price Android tier lower, (c) build a dedicated app.

### Risk 7: Nobody Discovers the Breadcrumbs (MEDIUM)
The clandestine campaign assumes people will connect oBitchuary → Shit Eyes → Hot Ghost → dezibel. Research shows literary fiction readers don't behave like horror fans (decode+share).
**Mitigation:** Don't depend on breadcrumbs for acquisition. Use them for depth/cult status. Acquisition comes from press, trailer, Lovense App Gallery, word of mouth.

### Risk 8: Self-Funding Doesn't Arrive by Month 6 (MEDIUM)
If subscriber growth is slower than projected, the $700K runs out before revenue covers costs.
**Mitigation:** Budget for 9 months of burn, not 6. The contingency exists for this. If Month 4 data is below 5,000 subs, reduce burn aggressively (pause non-essential ecosystem spend).
