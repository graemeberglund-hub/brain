## Thesis

The anti-algorithm angle is real and supported by data (63% Gen Z planned social media detox, Lush/Patagonia precedents). But "anti-X" positioning is reactive — it defines the product by what it's against, not what it is. Dezibel should lead with what it offers ("fiction that respects your time," "a story that arrives when it arrives") and let the anti-algorithm angle emerge as subtext. Aspirational framing attracts; oppositional framing repels the mainstream.

## Evidence For

- Category creation research: successful category creators name what they ARE, not what they're against
- Calm didn't launch as "anti-anxiety" — it launched as "calm"
- Wordle wasn't "anti-infinite-scroll" — it was "one puzzle a day"
- Research finding: "the framing should be aspirational, not oppositional"
- [[kill-test-full-project]] — validates format as "genuinely new, not just new for publishing," supporting aspirational framing (2026-03-26)

## Evidence Against

- Oppositional positioning gets press attention faster (controversy = coverage)
- "Anti-Netflix" is a more memorable pitch than "temporal fiction"
- Lush and Patagonia succeeded with explicitly oppositional messaging
