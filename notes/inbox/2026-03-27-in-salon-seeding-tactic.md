Tactic: Send free dezibel experience codes as beautifully designed cards to hair salons in LA and NYC.

## Why salons

- Captive dwell time (30-90 min, phone in hand)
- Stylists are professional conversationalists — they seed to every client
- Taste-forward demographic — people who care about culture and aesthetics
- Physical social space — a card on the counter gets seen by everyone
- If the card is designed well enough, it gets photographed before the code is redeemed

## Requirements

- Card design must feel like an object, not a flyer — something a stylist keeps on the mirror
- Copywriting powerful enough to inspire commitment from a cold audience
- Free code removes price barrier for first experience

## Cost estimate (rough)

- Design + print + distribution to 200 salons in LA + NYC: likely under $5K
- High ROI if even 10% of recipients activate and talk about it

## Behavioral mechanic

Maps to "scarcity as social currency" and "format as experience" from the unconventional marketing research. The card is a physical artifact that creates curiosity in a social setting. The salon is the decode space — people ask "what is this?" and the conversation IS the marketing.
