## Thesis

Between 2021 and 2025, three major companies spent $1.55B acquiring or building serialized fiction platforms built on the library/micropayment model. All three failed:

- **Kakao → Radish Fiction ($440M, 2021):** Shut down December 2025. $325M goodwill writedown. Unprofitable at scale.
- **Naver → Wattpad ($600M, 2021):** MAU declining 10.5% YoY. Not a meaningful revenue driver inside Webtoon's $1.35B business. Part of a combined $1.55B Naver/Kakao spending spree on US storytelling platforms that produced no profitable standalone product.
- **Amazon → Kindle Vella (internal, 2021):** Shut down February 2025. Amazon's own statement: "hasn't caught on as we'd hoped."

The failure mode is identical in all three: ad-supported or micropayment-gated content libraries competing on volume. More content, lower price, race to commodity. The model requires massive MAU to generate meaningful revenue, and even at 90M+ users (Wattpad) the economics don't work.

One company broke the pattern: **Quinn.** $3.2M raised. $11M+ ARR by August 2024. 440% YoY growth for two consecutive years. Premium subscription. Female-gaze. Audio-first. Single-creator feel. No micropayments. No ads. No library sprawl.

Quinn proved the market wants premium experience, not discounted volume.

## What This Means for Dezibel

Dezibel is structurally on the Quinn side of this divide:

| | Dead Model (Radish/Vella/Wattpad) | Working Model (Quinn) | Dezibel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing | Micropayments / free+ads | Premium subscription | Experience pricing ($49-199) |
| Content | Library (thousands of titles) | Curated library | Single authored work |
| Moat | Volume (no moat) | Brand + quality | Format + multi-sensory + scarcity |
| Revenue per user | Low (pennies/session) | ~$10-15/month | $49-199 one-time |
| Delivery | App reads | App listens | App + SMS + audio + haptic |

The graveyard proves the negative: volume serialized fiction does not produce viable businesses even with $600M acquisitions and 90M users. Quinn proves the positive: premium, opinionated, female-audience content does.

Dezibel extends Quinn's thesis into new territory — from audio-only to multi-sensory, from library to single-title experience, from monthly subscription to event pricing. The risk is execution, not market. The market has already voted.

## For the Deck

This is a thesis slide, not a TAM slide. The framing:

> "$1.55B was spent proving the wrong model. The right model costs $3.2M and generates $11M ARR at 440% growth. We're building on the model that works."

Spend 15 seconds on TAM/SAM/SOM as scaffolding. Spend 2 minutes on the graveyard and why the failure pattern doesn't apply. The number investors remember is "$440M acquisition shut down in 4 years" — not a market-size circle.

## Kill Test

This thesis dies if: (1) a new micropayment/library platform achieves profitability at scale, proving the model works with better execution, or (2) Quinn's growth reverses, suggesting the premium model was a timing artifact rather than a structural advantage. Neither has occurred as of April 2026.
