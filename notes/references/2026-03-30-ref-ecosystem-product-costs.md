# Kill Test K36: Ecosystem Product Costs

**Question:** Does $16-25K cover all five ecosystem products at minimum viable quality?

**Method:** Web research across manufacturer pricing, engineer rate surveys, auction records, wholesale distributors, and distribution platform comparisons. Prices reflect 2025-2026 market rates for small-run / indie-scale production.

---

## 1. Hot Ghost Incense — Custom Incense Brand

### Budget assumption: $3-5K

### Research findings

**Manufacturing (product itself):**
- Private label incense at 200-500 unit scale: $0.05-0.22 per stick depending on quality tier and customization
- A package of ~20 sticks (standard retail incense box): raw product cost $1-4.40/package
- At 200 packages: product cost = $200-880
- At 500 packages: product cost = $500-2,200
- US-based private label manufacturers (Incense Collective, Natural Choice Incense, Aroma Warehouse) offer small-batch runs with no prohibitive MOQ
- Alibaba manufacturers accept MOQ of 200 units but quality control is harder; US/Canadian manufacturers preferred for brand credibility
- Production timeline: 4-8 weeks after fragrance selection and proof approval

**Packaging (custom branded boxes):**
- Custom incense packaging at 200-500 units: $1.50-4.00/box depending on material and print complexity
- Embossing, foil stamping, or specialty finishes add $0.50-2.00/unit
- Design: if done in-house or by brand designer already on payroll, $0. Standalone packaging design commission: $500-1,500
- Total packaging at 300 units: $450-1,800

**Shipping (manufacturer to you):**
- Domestic US shipping for 200-500 packages: $100-300
- If shipping from overseas manufacturer: $300-800

**Retail placement (Brooklyn stores):**
- Consignment terms: stores typically take 40-60% of retail price. No upfront cost to place product.
- Wholesale terms: stores buy at 50% of retail. At $22 retail, wholesale = $11/unit. You get paid upfront but carry inventory risk.
- Travel to NYC for placement: separate from product budget (marketing line item)
- Target stores: Mast Books, Catbird, Brooklyn Candle Studio retail partners, independent apothecaries on Atlantic Ave / Smith St

**Total realistic cost at 300 units:**

| Line item | Low | High |
|-----------|-----|------|
| Product (300 packages × 20 sticks) | $300 | $1,320 |
| Custom packaging (300 units) | $450 | $1,200 |
| Packaging design | $0 | $1,500 |
| Shipping | $100 | $300 |
| Samples from 3 manufacturers | $100 | $300 |
| **Total** | **$950** | **$4,620** |

**Timeline:** 8-12 weeks from first sample order to finished product in hand.

### Verdict on budget assumption

**$3-5K is REALISTIC.** Likely lands at $2-4K if packaging design is handled by the brand designer already budgeted. The high end ($4,620) only hits if you go premium packaging with standalone design commission. At 200 units, costs drop further ($700-3,200). Consignment placement costs $0 upfront.

## 2. Shit Eyes Album — Mixing, Mastering, Distribution

### Budget assumption: $5-8K

### Research findings

**Mixing (8-10 tracks, mid-tier engineer):**
- Mid-tier mix engineers (5-10 years experience, proper studio): $200-500/track
- Album discount (10+ tracks): 15-20% off per-track rate
- 10 tracks at $350/track with 15% album discount: $2,975
- Range for 8-10 tracks: $1,920-4,250 after discount

**Mastering (8-10 tracks):**
- Mid-tier mastering: $50-100/track
- Flat album rate (common at indie level): $500-2,000
- 10 tracks at $75/track: $750
- Range for 8-10 tracks: $400-1,000 per-track; or $500-2,000 flat album rate

**Distribution:**
- DistroKid: $22.99/year, unlimited uploads, keep 100% of royalties. Best for dezibel's use case (album is world-building, not revenue play — minimal ongoing cost)
- TuneCore: moved to subscription model in 2025, competitive with DistroKid
- CD Baby: $29 one-time per album, no annual fees, but takes 9% commission
- **Recommendation stands: DistroKid at $22.99/year.** One-time setup, negligible cost.

**Spotify Canvas portraits (Grady Mitchell):**
- Already budgeted separately at ~$1K (noted in ecosystem strategy doc)
- Not included in this line item

**Total realistic cost:**

| Line item | Low | High |
|-----------|-----|------|
| Mixing (10 tracks, mid-tier) | $1,920 | $4,250 |
| Mastering (10 tracks) | $500 | $2,000 |
| Distribution (DistroKid annual) | $23 | $23 |
| **Total** | **$2,443** | **$6,273** |

**Timeline:** Mixing: 2-4 weeks per album (depends on engineer availability and revision rounds). Mastering: 1-2 weeks. Distribution: live on platforms within 1-2 weeks of upload.

### Verdict on budget assumption

**$5-8K is REALISTIC but slightly high for mid-tier.** The sweet spot is $3-5K for solid mid-tier work. $7-9K (the existing budget line) would get you into the upper-mid range — an engineer with real credits and a proper analog chain. Not necessary if the album is atmospheric world-building rather than a standalone commercial release. At $5-6K you get professional quality that holds up on streaming.

**Recommendation: budget $5-6K, not $7-9K.** Savings of $2-3K can shift to contingency.

## 3. Pyrrha Charm — Custom Jewelry Collaboration

### Budget assumption: $2-4K (or $0 if collaboration)

### Research findings

**Pyrrha's collaboration model:**
- Pyrrha has done IP collaborations with HBO (Game of Thrones, 2014), Universal Pictures (Wicked, 2024), and author V.E. Schwab
- In the HBO deal, Pyrrha was approached by HBO licensing executives — Pyrrha produces and sells through their own channels + retail partners
- Standard model: Pyrrha designs, manufactures, and distributes. The IP holder provides creative direction and receives a licensing royalty (industry standard: 5-10% of net sales)
- Pyrrha retail price range: $98-$900 (sterling silver and bronze talismans). Wicked collection: $325-2,895.
- Pyrrha is a certified B Corp, manufactures in Vancouver from reclaimed metals

**Two possible structures for dezibel:**

**(A) Revenue-share collaboration (preferred):**
- Dezibel provides the lemon/rhododendron motif and story context
- Pyrrha designs and produces through their existing infrastructure
- Pyrrha sells through their channels (website, 81K Instagram, retail partners)
- Dezibel receives 5-8% royalty on net sales
- **Dezibel cost: $0 upfront**
- Revenue to dezibel: at $325/charm × 200 units = $65K gross. Your royalty: $3,250-5,200
- Marketing value: Pyrrha's audience + press story ("Vancouver jewelry brand creates talisman for erotic novel")

**(B) Commission (dezibel pays for production):**
- Pyrrha designs and produces a custom run for dezibel to sell directly
- Custom sterling silver charm production: $40-80/unit manufacturing cost at Pyrrha's scale
- 200 units: $8,000-16,000 manufacturing cost
- Dezibel sells at $150-325 retail
- **This is expensive and unnecessary unless Pyrrha won't do revenue share**

**Timeline:** Pyrrha's design process: 4-8 weeks for design approval. Production: 4-6 weeks. Total: 8-14 weeks.

### Verdict on budget assumption

**$0 is the correct budget if revenue-share collaboration works.** This is the most likely structure given Pyrrha's track record with IP collaborations (HBO, Universal, V.E. Schwab). Wade Papin is the founder and a direct contact — this is not a cold pitch. The $2-4K budget assumption is only relevant if Pyrrha asks for a design fee, which is unlikely given their collaboration model. **Budget $0, with $2K contingency earmarked.**

## 4. Andy Dixon Cover Art — Commissioned Painting

### Budget assumption: $3-5K

### Research findings

**Andy Dixon market pricing:**
- Auction record: $22,472 USD (Sotheby's Hong Kong, 2022, "Lord Berners Faringdon House" via Christie's)
- Auction range: $252-$22,472 depending on size and medium
- Gallery representation: Over the Influence (LA/Hong Kong), The Hole (NYC), Opera Gallery (London, 2026)
- Notable: Versace collaboration (paintings transformed into fashion collection, 2019-2020)
- Museum collections: Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art, West Collection at SEI
- Based in Los Angeles (born Vancouver)

**Primary market vs. commission pricing:**
- Gallery primary market prices for Dixon's scale of work: estimated $10,000-25,000+ for a large canvas (based on auction records and gallery tier)
- Commercial/album art commission: typically 30-60% of fine art pricing for artists at this level, because the work is purpose-made and rights may be shared
- "Friend rate" for someone like Graeme (who knows Andy personally): potentially significant discount
- Alternative structure: reduced commission fee + profit-sharing on prints/merchandise featuring the art

**What you're actually buying:**
- One painting for album cover art (Shit Eyes)
- Potentially secondary use as story art / premium edition art
- Reproduction rights (critical — needs to be in the contract)

**Realistic cost scenarios:**

| Scenario | Cost | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Full market commission | $10,000-25,000 | What a stranger would pay through gallery |
| Friend rate commission | $5,000-10,000 | Reduced fee, personal relationship |
| Friend rate + print profit share | $3,000-7,000 | Lower upfront, Andy participates in upside |
| Art trade / collaboration | $0-3,000 | If Andy sees value in association with project |

### Verdict on budget assumption

**$3-5K is OPTIMISTIC but potentially achievable given the friendship.** Market rate for a Dixon commission is $10-25K. The existing budget note ($5-10K, lean on friendship) is more realistic. At $3-5K you're asking for a significant friend discount — possible but depends on the relationship and Andy's interest in the project. **Budget $5-8K to be safe. Structure as reduced fee + print profit share to get the upfront cost down.**

**Risk flag:** If Andy's gallery has first right of refusal on commissions, the gallery may add 50% to whatever Andy quotes. Confirm whether this is a direct-to-artist deal or goes through gallery.

## 5. T-Shirts — 3 Designs, 150 Units

### Budget assumption: $3,600-4,500

### Research findings

**Blank t-shirt cost (LA Apparel 1801GD garment-dyed):**
- Wholesale price: ~$9.46-12.00/unit depending on distributor and quantity
- At 150 units: ~$1,419-1,800 for blanks alone
- LA Apparel is premium — Gildan or Bella+Canvas blanks run $3-6/unit but lower quality

**Screen printing cost (150 units, 3 designs = 50/design):**
- Setup fee: $25/color/screen. At 2 colors × 3 designs = $150 in setup fees
- Print cost at 50 units/design: $5-10/shirt (small run, higher per-unit)
- Total print cost: 150 × $7.50 avg = $1,125
- Note: 50 units per design is a small run — you're not hitting the bulk price breaks (which start at 100+). Consider 75/design (225 total) if budget allows.

**Total cost breakdown:**

| Line item | Low | High |
|-----------|-----|------|
| Blanks (150 × LA Apparel) | $1,419 | $1,800 |
| Screen printing (150 units, 3 designs, 2 colors) | $900 | $1,500 |
| Setup fees (3 designs × 2 colors) | $150 | $225 |
| Design (if not done by brand designer) | $0 | $500 |
| **Total** | **$2,469** | **$4,025** |

**Break-even math:**
- At $70 retail price and $24-27 total cost/shirt: margin = $43-46/shirt
- Break-even: 54-60 units sold (36-40% sell-through)
- Full sell-through revenue: 150 × $70 = $10,500
- Profit at full sell-through: $6,475-8,031

**Timeline:** Blanks: 1-2 weeks to order. Screen printing: 1-2 weeks production. Total: 2-4 weeks.

### Verdict on budget assumption

**$3,600-4,500 is REALISTIC and well-researched.** Actual cost likely lands at $2,500-4,000 depending on blank choice and print complexity. The budget assumption has appropriate headroom. This is the lowest-risk ecosystem product — commodity execution with known margins.

## VERDICT

**$16-25K covers all five ecosystem products.** The total research estimate of $10,862-24,918 falls within the budgeted range.

**Where the budget is tight:** Andy Dixon is the one line item that could blow past estimates. If the friendship discount doesn't materialize and this goes through a gallery, you're looking at $10-25K for that single item — which alone would eat half the ecosystem budget.

**Where the budget has slack:**
- Pyrrha at $0 (revenue share) saves $2-4K vs assumption
- Shit Eyes at $5-6K vs $7-9K budgeted saves $2-3K
- Hot Ghost likely comes in at $2-3K, not $5K

**Net position:** The savings on Pyrrha, Shit Eyes, and Hot Ghost (~$4-7K) roughly offset the risk on Andy Dixon. The budget holds IF the Dixon commission stays under $8K.

**Kill test result: PASS.** The $16-25K ecosystem budget is sufficient for all five products at minimum viable quality. No single product has a cost structure that makes it infeasible at this scale.

### Contingency recommendations

1. **Lock Andy Dixon terms early.** This is the only variable that could blow the budget. Get a verbal commitment on price before committing other ecosystem spend.
2. **Order Hot Ghost samples now.** $100-300 to validate quality and manufacturer. Cheapest kill test in the set.
3. **Contact Wade Papin now.** Confirm revenue-share structure before budgeting any Pyrrha spend.
4. **Mix/master the album at $5-6K, not $7-9K.** Mid-tier is sufficient for a world-building asset. Save the delta for contingency.
5. **Consider 225 t-shirts (75/design) instead of 150.** Per-unit cost drops meaningfully at higher quantity, and you have more inventory to sell through the ecosystem.
