### Dezibel: Adversarial Audit + Cross-System Research (dezibel-editor)

Ran kill test against the live iteration system — 5 CRITICAL findings: statistical validity breaks at small cohort sizes (n=50-200 can't detect 5% changes), operational feasibility requires 3-5 people not one, screenshot detection is a surveillance headline, the methodology moat is the document itself, and author is single point of failure for every content change. Cross-referenced against 10 real systems (YouTube, Spotify, Duolingo, Peloton, Calm, Episode Interactive, Kakao/Webtoon, Substack, Apple Podcasts, academic research). Identified 8 unsolved industry gaps — most critical: no one has built within-installment engagement tracking for push-delivered text, narrative-aware A/B testing, or small-cohort statistical methods for content iteration.

### Dezibel: Micro-Participation Research (dezibel-editor)

Researched 15 examples of passive media with micro-participation mechanics — from Bandersnatch (Netflix abandoned full branching) to Sleep No More (selection-as-participation) to Spotify Wrapped (retroactive participation, 200M users in 24h) to Dora the Explorer (ask, pause, continue regardless). Core finding: micro-participation works when it changes the reader, not the story. Seven design principles emerged. The strongest mechanic for Dezibel may be the reflection prompt in the gap between installments, not in-stream choices.

### Dezibel: Invizibel Community Design (dezibel-editor)

Designed and rewrote the Invizibel community system — the reader discussion space for Dezibel. New cohort every Sunday, 52/year, each with its own spoiler-protected space lasting 6 weeks. Weekly discussion topics tied to story beats. Graduated cohorts move to a permanent alumni space. Researched cohort precedents (Maven 96% W1→W2 retention, Peloton presence indicators, Strava single-tap engagement), kill-tested character accounts (CRITICAL risk — removed entirely), and corrected scale from 50/cohort to thousands/week. Key insight: the community doubles as a real-time feedback loop — every cohort is a continuous focus group on what lands and what doesn't, compounding across 52 cohorts/year. The story is the product; Invizibel is where readers go to talk about it.
