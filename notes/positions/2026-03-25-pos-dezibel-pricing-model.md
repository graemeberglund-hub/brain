## Thesis

Dezibel should price as an experience ($49-199), not as content ($5-15). The 42-day SMS delivery, audio performances, haptic sync, and time-gated pacing create an experience that has no content-priced comparable. Pricing against books ($15-30) or subscriptions ($5-15/month) undervalues the product and makes the $100M target impossible. Pricing against experiences (escape rooms $30-50, immersive theater $75-300, masterclasses $50-200) positions correctly.

## Evidence For

- [[kill-test-full-project]] — A1 finds peak writing "genuinely exceptional," conditionally supports experience pricing (2026-03-26)
- Marketing research: experience pricing comparables support $50-200 range
- Unit economics: 90%+ margins at all price points — floor is demand, not cost
- Cohort-based delivery creates scarcity (limited starts, fixed dates) which supports premium pricing
- No direct comparable exists — first-mover gets to set the anchor
- Superhuman precedent: $30/month email client succeeded by pricing as premium experience, not commodity

## Evidence Against

- [[kill-test-full-project]] — A8 finds $49 UNVERIFIED: no A/B test, no WTP survey, no precedent for SMS fiction pricing (2026-03-26)
- No market validation yet — 0 people have paid for dezibel at any price
- SMS fiction precedents (Hooked, Kindle Vella) priced as content and still struggled
- $49+ for a "book" may create sticker shock regardless of framing
- Risk of pricing out the discovery audience that generates word-of-mouth
