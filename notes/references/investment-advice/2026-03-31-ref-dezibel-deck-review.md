## Overview

Critical review of all three Dezibel investor deck versions (Canva-built PDFs). The decks iterate on the same core structure. Reviewed against the current working state in `dezibel-editor/strategy/plan-to-launch.md` and `brain/notes/projects/dezibel.md`. Context: $700-850K raise target, no meetings booked yet, no lawyer engaged.

## What Works

- **Bio is genuinely impressive.** Cheaper Show (10K attendees, Western Canada's largest annual art show), Audain collection, Chip Wilson advisory, Vancouver Art Gallery curation, Yellowjackets/A24 placements. Establishes execution track record at scale.
- **Ecosystem products are the strongest differentiator.** Pyrrha (Taylor Swift, Al Pacino), Andy Dixon (Kanye, Versace), Eugenio Battaliaga (Heretic, Longlegs). Real names, real audiences, real products that exist inside and outside the story. This is what makes Dezibel investable beyond "first-time novelist."
- **The concept is genuinely novel.** "First literary novel delivered by iMessage" is a category-creating sentence. The medium-as-message thesis is sound.

## Critical Problems

### 1. No Financial Information (SEVERITY: BLOCKING)

The deck contains zero financial content:
- No raise amount stated
- No budget breakdown or use of funds
- No return structure (equity? revenue share? convertible note?)
- No revenue projections or unit economics
- No subscriber scenario modeling

The plan-to-launch.md and budget-realistic.md contain all this thinking. The deck has none of it. An investor cannot write a check without knowing what they're buying.

**Fix:** Add use-of-funds slide ($700K breakdown), revenue model slide ($49/$149/$199 tiers × subscriber scenarios), and investment structure slide.

### 2. 50 Shades Comparison (SEVERITY: HIGH)

"We are aiming to create a similar phenomenon" and "creating a global sensation in 2026." 50 Shades sold 153M copies through Random House. Dezibel is a first-time author self-publishing via SMS. The comparison invites the wrong question ("Why would this reach that scale?") and reads as naive ambition to sophisticated investors.

What Dezibel actually has is better than a 50 Shades comparison: a new medium, a new business model, and a creator ecosystem that doesn't exist anywhere else.

**Fix:** Kill the 50 Shades framing entirely. Lead with category creation and the ecosystem moat.

### 3. Timeline Is Fiction (SEVERITY: HIGH)

All versions show Fall 2025 or specific dates (Nov, Dec, Jan, April) that have passed. It's March 2026. Investor immediately questions organizational capacity.

**Fix:** Update to realistic dates or remove timeline entirely and discuss verbally.

### 4. "Most Talked About Piece of Media in 2026" (SEVERITY: MEDIUM-HIGH)

This slide title appears in all versions. Unfalsifiable claim that sets expectations you'll be measured against. Undermines credibility.

**Fix:** Replace with a framing of actual competitive advantage: the medium, the ecosystem, the intimacy model.

### 5. Marketing Plan Is Aspirational (SEVERITY: MEDIUM-HIGH)

- "Expected reach of 250,000-500,000+ unique profiles" — no influencers named, no follower counts, no conversion math.
- "Gifting codes to influencers" — which influencers?
- Step 4 (International expansion: Mandarin, Spanish, Japanese) should not appear in a $700K raise deck. Signals thinking about Tokyo before selling 500 copies in Vancouver.

**Fix:** Show actual funnel math (the Guardian piece math from plan-to-launch.md is real and credible). Cut international expansion. Name the actual channels with real numbers.

### 6. Too Much shit_eyes Content (SEVERITY: MEDIUM)

Two full slides on Graeme's music career. In a $700K novel fundraise, this creates "is this person focused?" doubt. The album matters as ecosystem — it doesn't need its own origin story.

**Fix:** Consolidate to one paragraph on the ecosystem slide alongside Pyrrha, Dixon, Eugenio, oBitchuary, Hot Ghost.

### 7. Roles/Hires Red Flag (SEVERITY: MEDIUM)

14+ roles listed (Author, Advisors, Editor, PM, Designer, Coder/CTO, Brand Manager, Book Cover Designer, Props Master, UX/UI, Casting Director, Market Research, PR Agent, Marketing Agency, Poetry Sourcing). For $700K, this reads like building a 15-person company for a text novel. Investor does burn-rate math and panics.

**Fix:** Show 4-5 critical hires with costs. Everything else is contract/vendor work — frame it that way.

### 8. XXX Talent Placeholders (SEVERITY: MEDIUM)

Three blank "XXX" slots on the talent page. Undermines the one confirmed name (Kaylah Zander).

**Fix:** Either show confirmed cast only (Kaylah, Edo, Madison, Leda) or save casting for verbal pitch.

### 9. Typo on Slide 1 (SEVERITY: LOW but embarrassing)

"The first of it's kind" → should be "its." First slide, first impression.

## Recommended Deck Structure (12 slides)

1. **Cover** — Dezibel. A novel that texts you back.
2. **The concept** — one slide, one paragraph, one screenshot of iMessage
3. **How it works** — reader journey, Day 1 → Day 42
4. **What exists today** — demo, manuscript, album, talent (show progress, not just vision)
5. **The creator** — bio condensed to one slide
6. **The market** — erotic fiction + interactive media opportunity, without 50 Shades comparison
7. **The ecosystem** — Pyrrha, Dixon, Eugenio, oBitchuary, Hot Ghost, shit_eyes — one slide, all of them
8. **Revenue model** — tiers, unit economics, subscriber scenarios at 3 levels
9. **Use of funds** — $700K breakdown (pie chart or table)
10. **The team** — 4-5 key hires, not 14
11. **Timeline** — realistic, updated to current reality
12. **The ask** — what you're raising, what the investor gets, investment structure

## Deck vs. Meeting Strategy

The deck is not the pitch — the demo is. Recommended meeting flow:
1. **90-second video** → gets the meeting
2. **Deck** → frames the business (5 minutes in the room)
3. **Live 25-minute demo** → the actual pitch (they feel it on their phone)
4. **Conversation** → the ask

The deck's job is to survive being forwarded. It needs to work without you in the room explaining it. Right now it doesn't — the financial and structural gaps mean a forwarded deck generates questions, not checks.
